Frequently Asked Questions

A sump pump is a device used to remove water that has accumulated in a water-collecting sump basin, commonly found in the basements of homes. It helps prevent flooding and water damage by directing water away from your home, particularly in areas with a high water table or high levels of rainfall.

The size of the sump pump you need depends on various factors including the size of your basement, the amount of water you anticipate handling, and the vertical lift required to discharge the water. Itâ€™s generally advisable to consult with a professional to ensure you select the appropriate pump with the right capacity and horsepower.

While it's possible to install a sump pump yourself if you have basic plumbing skills and tools, it's often recommended to hire a professional. Proper installation involves several steps including setting up drainage, installing check valves, and possibly electrical wiring to ensure the pump operates correctly and safely.

A sump pump should be tested at least once every few months to ensure it is functioning properly. Regular maintenance includes checking the pump's power source, cleaning the pump itself, and ensuring that the discharge line is clear of obstructions. It is also advisable to have a professional inspection annually.

Signs that your sump pump needs replacement include unusual noises, constant running, visible rust or corrosion, infrequent water discharge, or if it's more than 7-10 years old. Overheating and cycling on and off too quickly can also indicate a failing pump.
