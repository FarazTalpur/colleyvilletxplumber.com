Frequently Asked Questions

Kitchen drains often clog due to a buildup of grease, soap, food particles, and sometimes foreign objects that get lodged in the pipes.

You can clean your kitchen drain naturally by pouring a mixture of equal parts baking soda and vinegar down the drain, letting it fizz for about 15 minutes, then flushing it with hot water.

Yes, boiling water can help dissolve mild clogs caused by grease or soap scum. Pour the boiling water directly into the drain slowly and in stages to avoid splashing.

Use a plunger when there is a significant clog that isn't cleared by using baking soda and vinegar. Ensure there is enough water in the sink to cover the plunger's head for effective suction.

While chemical drain cleaners can be effective, they can also be harmful to your pipes and the environment, and should be used as a last resort. Consider using them sparingly and with caution.
